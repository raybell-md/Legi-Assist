1 (a) An employer may not:

2 (1) fail or refuse to hire, discharge, or otherwise discriminate against any
3 individual with respect to the individual’s compensation, terms, conditions, or privileges of
4 employment because of:

5 (i) the individual’s race, color, religion, sex, age, national origin,
6 marital status, CAREGIVER STATUS, sexual orientation, gender identity, genetic
7 information, military status, or disability unrelated in nature and extent so as to
8 reasonably preclude the performance of the employment; or

9 (ii) the individual’s refusal to submit to a genetic test or make
10 available the results of a genetic test;

11 (2) limit, segregate, or classify its employees or applicants for employment
12 in any way that would deprive or tend to deprive any individual of employment
13 opportunities or otherwise adversely affect the individual’s status as an employee because
14 of:

15 (i) the individual’s race, color, religion, sex, age, national origin,
16 marital status, CAREGIVER STATUS, sexual orientation, gender identity, genetic
17 information, military status, or disability unrelated in nature and extent so as to

23 (4) fail or refuse to make a reasonable accommodation for the known
