1 AN ACT concerning

2 Maryland Military Department – National Guard – Tuition Assistance

3 FOR the purpose of expanding the institutions and types of expenses in connection with
4 which tuition assistance may be provided for certain eligible Maryland National
5 Guard service members; and generally relating to tuition assistance for members of
6 the Maryland National Guard.

14 Article – Public Safety

15 13–405.

19 community college established under Title 16 of the Education Article, Morgan State

22 a tuition waiver of at least 50%;

1 (iii) any public postsecondary vocational–technical or trade school; or

2 (iv) any private postsecondary vocational–technical [or trade school
3 that grants a member a tuition waiver of at least 50%], TRADE, OR CAREER SCHOOL
4 RECOGNIZED BY THE MARYLAND HIGHER EDUCATION COMMISSION OR THE
5 MARYLAND APPRENTICESHIP AND TRAINING COUNCIL.

6 (3) “Member” means an individual who:

7 (i) is regularly enlisted in the National Guard; or

8 (ii) holds a commission in the National Guard.

9 (4) (I) “TRAINING ASSISTANCE” MEANS EXPENSES DIRECTLY
10 RELATED TO PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT RESOURCES REQUIRED FOR
11 PARTICIPATION IN A COURSE OR PROGRAM AT AN INSTITUTION.

12 (II) “TRAINING ASSISTANCE” INCLUDES:

13 1. INDUSTRY–REQUIRED TOOLS, TECHNOLOGY, AND
14 PROFESSIONAL EQUIPMENT;

15 2. PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT, UNIFORMS,
16 AND SPECIALIZED CLOTHING;

17 3. CERTIFICATION AND LICENSING COSTS NECESSARY
18 FOR EMPLOYMENT; AND

19 4. TRADE–RELATED PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT
20 MATERIALS, SOFTWARE, AND DIGITAL RESOURCES.

21 (5) (i) “Tuition” means the basic instructional charge for
22 undergraduate, graduate, professional, vocational–technical, and trade school credit
23 courses and related fees at an institution.

24 (ii) “Tuition” does not include charges for self–supporting programs.

25 (b) (1) To the extent that funds are provided in the State budget, the
26 Department may provide assistance equal to 100% of the cost of in–State tuition AND
27 TRAINING ASSISTANCE for any regularly scheduled undergraduate credit course,
28 graduate credit course, professional credit course, vocational–technical course, or trade
29 course for any active member attending an institution who is certified as eligible by the
30 Adjutant General.

1 (2) Subject to paragraph (5) of this subsection, a member who receives
2 assistance under paragraph (1) of this subsection for an undergraduate credit,
3 vocational–technical, or trade OR CAREER course shall remain an active member for at
4 least 2 years following the completion of the course.

5 (3) Subject to paragraph (5) of this subsection, a member who receives
6 assistance under paragraph (1) of this subsection for a graduate or professional credit
7 course shall remain an active member for at least 4 years following the completion of the
8 course.

9 (4) Subject to paragraph (5) of this subsection, if a member receives
10 assistance under paragraph (1) of this subsection, and is a member of a unit that has been
11 disbanded on or after September 1, 2013, due to budgetary cuts, Base Realignment and
12 Closure, or any other reason, the member may satisfy the requirements of paragraph (2) or
13 paragraph (3) of this subsection by transferring to another active duty, reserve, or National
14 Guard Unit in the State or in another state.

15 (5) If a member who receives assistance under paragraph (1) of this
16 subsection is offered early separation by the military following the disbanding of the
17 member’s unit due to budget cuts, Base Realignment and Closure, or any other reason, the
18 member is excused from the requirements of paragraph (2) or paragraph (3) of this
19 subsection.

20 (c) (1) The Adjutant General may not certify a member as eligible unless the
21 member is:

22 (i) enlisted and has at least 24 months remaining to serve on the
23 current enlistment of the member; or

24 (ii) an officer or warrant officer and agrees in writing to serve for a
25 minimum of 24 months.

26 (2) The 24–month requirement runs from the first day of classes for the
27 semester.

28 (d) If a recipient of tuition assistance under this section is discharged from the
29 National Guard for a reason designated by the Adjutant General, the assistance terminates
30 and the member shall reimburse the Department the amount of tuition assistance received
31 for that semester within 30 days of discharge.

32 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
33 October 1, 2026.
