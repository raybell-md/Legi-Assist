A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Commemorative Months – Maryland Climate Education Month

3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Governor annually to proclaim April as Maryland Climate
4 Education Month; requiring the proclamation to urge participation in certain
5 activities and initiatives and the taking of certain actions; and generally relating to
6 Maryland Climate Education Month.

8 Article – General Provisions

18 Preamble

19 WHEREAS, Maryland has a long history of environmental stewardship and a
20 commitment to protecting the natural beauty and resources of the State; and

21 WHEREAS, Climate change poses a significant threat to our environment, economy,
22 and public health; and

23 WHEREAS, Maryland is uniquely positioned to lead in climate change education as
24 a state that possesses diverse ecosystems, vulnerable coastal areas, and the Chesapeake
25 Bay, all of which are directly impacted by climate pollution; and

2 HOUSE BILL 620

1 WHEREAS, Education plays a vital role in raising awareness about climate change
2 and its causes, consequences, and solutions; and

3 WHEREAS, Institutions of higher education, primary and secondary schools,
4 research organizations, and environmental and advocacy groups in the State are at the
5 forefront of climate change research and education and are the State’s partners in this
6 critical effort; and

7 WHEREAS, Maryland is dedicated to fostering a culture of sustainability and
8 environmental responsibility among its residents, businesses, and institutions; and

9 WHEREAS, Maryland leads the nation in its efforts to reduce greenhouse gas
10 emissions that cause climate pollution; now, therefore,

11 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
12 That Section(s) 7–504 through 7–507 of Article – General Provisions of the Annotated Code
13 of Maryland be renumbered to be Section(s) 7–505 through 7–508, respectively.

14 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
15 as follows:

16 Article – General Provisions

17 7–504.

18 (A) THE GOVERNOR ANNUALLY SHALL PROCLAIM APRIL AS MARYLAND
19 CLIMATE EDUCATION MONTH.

20 (B) THE PROCLAMATION SHALL URGE RESIDENTS OF ALL AGES TO:

21 (1) PARTICIPATE IN EDUCATIONAL ACTIVITIES AND INITIATIVES
22 THAT PROMOTE AWARENESS OF CLIMATE CHANGE; AND

23 (2) TAKE ACTION TOWARD THE STATE’S CLIMATE COMMITMENTS.

24 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
25 October 1, 2026.
