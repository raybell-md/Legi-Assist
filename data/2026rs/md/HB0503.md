A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Baltimore City Sheriff’s Office – Collective Bargaining – Compensation

3 FOR the purpose of including salary and wages in the compensation with regard to which
4 full–time sworn law enforcement officers and court security officers in Baltimore City
5 may collectively bargain; altering the person with whom the full–time sworn officers
6 and court security officers are to seek recognition for the purposes of collectively
7 bargaining concerning certain matters; specifying that the full–time sworn officers
8 and court security officers are to bargain in good faith with the Sheriff, or the
9 Sheriff’s designee, and Baltimore City, rather than either the Sheriff or the Sheriff’s
10 designee or Baltimore City based on the subject of the collective bargaining; and
11 generally relating to collective bargaining rights for employees in the Baltimore City
12 Sheriff’s Office.

1 (a) This section applies only in Baltimore City.

2 (i) (1) This subsection applies only to all full–time sworn law enforcement
3 officers who are deputy sheriffs at the rank of lieutenant or below and court security
4 officers.

5 (2) This subsection does not apply to the following employees in the
6 Sheriff’s Office:

7 (i) Sworn law enforcement officers in the Sheriff’s Office at a rank
8 of captain or above;

9 (ii) Employees in appointed positions;

10 (iii) Civilian merit system employees;

11 (iv) Full–time reduced hours employees;

12 (v) Part–time employees;

13 (vi) Contractual employees;

14 (vii) Temporary employees;

15 (viii) Emergency employees; or

16 (ix) Employees whose employment is administered under the
17 Baltimore City policies and procedures manual.

18 (3) (i) A deputy sheriff or a court security officer has the right to:

19 1. Take part in or refrain from taking part in forming,
20 joining, supporting, or participating in any employee organization or its lawful activities;

21 2. Be represented by an exclusive representative, if any, in
22 collective bargaining; and

23 3. Engage in other concerted activities for the purpose of
24 collective bargaining.

25 (ii) Full–time sworn law enforcement officers and court security
26 officers may seek recognition by the Sheriff or the Sheriff’s designee in order to organize
27 and bargain collectively in good faith WITH THE SHERIFF, OR THE SHERIFF’S
28 DESIGNEE, AND THE CITY concerning the following matters:
