A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Criminal Law – Third–Degree Sexual Offense – Burglary

3 FOR the purpose of establishing that engaging in sexual contact with another without the
4 consent of the other in connection with a first–, second–, or third–degree burglary
5 constitutes sexual offense in the third degree; altering the definitions of “tier I sex
6 offender”, “tier II sex offender”, and “tier III sex offender” applicable to provisions
7 relating to sex offender registration to include certain acts constituting sexual
8 offense in the third degree; and generally relating to sexual offense in the third
9 degree.

1 Article – Criminal Law

2 3–307.

3 (a) A person may not:

4 (1) (i) engage in sexual contact with another without the consent of the
5 other; and

6 (ii) 1. employ or display a dangerous weapon, or a physical object
7 that the victim reasonably believes is a dangerous weapon;

8 2. suffocate, strangle, disfigure, or inflict serious physical
9 injury on the victim or another in the course of committing the crime;

10 3. threaten, or place the victim in fear, that the victim, or an
11 individual known to the victim, imminently will be subject to death, suffocation,
12 strangulation, disfigurement, serious physical injury, or kidnapping; [or]

13 4. commit the crime while aided and abetted by another; OR

14 5. COMMIT THE CRIME IN CONNECTION WITH A
15 BURGLARY IN THE FIRST DEGREE UNDER § 6–202 OF THIS ARTICLE, A BURGLARY IN
16 THE SECOND DEGREE UNDER § 6–203 OF THIS ARTICLE, OR A BURGLARY IN THE
17 THIRD DEGREE UNDER § 6–204 OF THIS ARTICLE;

18 (2) engage in sexual contact with another if the victim is a substantially
19 cognitively impaired individual, a mentally incapacitated individual, or a physically
20 helpless individual, and the person performing the act knows or reasonably should know
21 the victim is a substantially cognitively impaired individual, a mentally incapacitated
22 individual, or a physically helpless individual;

23 (3) engage in sexual contact with another if the victim is under the age of
24 14 years, and the person performing the sexual contact is at least 4 years older than the
25 victim;

26 (4) engage in a sexual act with another if the victim is 14 or 15 years old,
27 and the person performing the sexual act is at least 21 years old; or

28 (5) engage in vaginal intercourse with another if the victim is 14 or 15
29 years old, and the person performing the act is at least 21 years old.

30 (b) A person who violates this section is guilty of the felony of sexual offense in
31 the third degree and on conviction is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 10 years.
