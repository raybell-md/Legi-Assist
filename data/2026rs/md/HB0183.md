1 (d) “Emissions equipment and misfueling inspection” means an inspection to
2 verify the presence of required emissions equipment and an inspection to determine that
3 the vehicle has not been misfueled.

4 (e) (1) “Emissions related repair” means the inspection, adjustment, repair, or
5 replacement of motor vehicle engine systems, subsystems, or components as necessary to
6 bring a motor vehicle into compliance with emissions standards adopted in accordance with
7 the provisions of this subtitle.

8 (2) “Emissions related repair” does not include adjustment, repair, or
9 replacement necessitated by tampering or misfueling.

10 (f) (1) “Emissions standard” means a requirement that limits the quantity,
11 quality, rate, or concentration of emissions from a motor vehicle.

12 (2) “Emissions standard” includes a requirement that relates to the
13 operation or maintenance of a motor vehicle to assure continuous emissions reduction.

14 (g) “Exhaust emissions test” means the sampling and measurement of certain
15 components of motor vehicle exhaust to determine whether the motor vehicle is in
16 compliance with an emissions standard.

17 (h) “Misfueling” means the introduction of leaded fuel into a motor vehicle
18 designed by the motor vehicle manufacturer to use unleaded fuel.

19 (i) “Secretary” means the Secretary of the Environment.]
