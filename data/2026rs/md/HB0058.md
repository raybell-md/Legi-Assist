A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Transportation – Paratransit Services – Interjurisdictional Routes

3 FOR the purpose of requiring a county that submits an application to the Secretary of
4 Transportation for funding for certain transportation services to identify certain
5 interjurisdictional paratransit service routes in the application; requiring the
6 Secretary to adopt procedures to ensure counties cooperate in establishing certain
7 interjurisdictional paratransit service routes; requiring a county that receives
8 funding for certain transportation services to establish certain interjurisdictional
9 paratransit service routes; and generally relating to interjurisdictional paratransit
10 service routes.

1 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.

2 (2) “County” includes Baltimore City.

3 (3) “Elderly and handicapped person” means any person who, by reason of
4 illness, injury, age, congenital malfunction, or other permanent or temporary incapacity or
5 disability, is unable to use mass transit facilities and services as effectively as a person who
6 is not so affected.

7 (4) “Transportation service” means any transportation option or mix of
8 options that may include paratransit or fixed route service, established or employed by a
9 county to accommodate the transportation needs of its elderly and handicapped residents.

10 (b) (1) (i) Subject to subparagraphs (ii) through (iv) of this paragraph, the
11 Secretary shall identify separately within the Department’s annual budget an amount to
12 be used for elderly and handicapped transportation service in each county.

13 (2) The amount the Secretary identifies, or such other amount as may be
14 appropriated by the General Assembly for the provision of elderly and handicapped
15 transportation service in each county, shall be distributed as provided in subsections (c),
16 (d), and (e) of this section.

17 (c) (1) Of the amount separately identified or otherwise appropriated under
18 subsection (b) of this section, the Secretary shall:

19 (i) Allocate 60 percent equally among the counties; and

20 (ii) Allocate the remaining amount among the counties in proportion
21 to their respective percentages of the State’s combined elderly and handicapped population.

22 (2) Subject to the limitations provided in subsection (f) of this section, the
23 Secretary shall distribute all such allocated amounts to those counties which file a written
24 application with the Department in such form and detail as the Secretary requires.

25 (3) A written application submitted by a county under this subsection may
26 not be accepted or considered by the Secretary unless [the]:

31 THOSE COUNTIES IN THE FOLLOWING NETWORKS OF PROVIDERS:

32 1. ADVENTIST HEALTHCARE;
