A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Earned Income Tax Credit – Assistance Program Implementation Delay and
3 Study

4 FOR the purpose of delaying the implementation of the Earned Income Tax Credit
5 Assistance Program until a certain taxable year; requiring the Comptroller’s Office,
6 on or before a certain date, to study and make recommendations regarding outreach
7 efforts to encourage eligible taxpayers to claim the State earned income tax credit;
8 requiring the Department of Service and Civic Innovation to make certain
9 recommendations concerning methods to assist low–income Marylanders with
10 claiming certain credits and obtaining certain assistance; and generally relating to
11 the State earned income tax credit.

2 (a) In this section, “Program” means the Maryland Earned Income Tax Credit
3 Assistance Program for Low–Income Families.

4 (b) (1) There is a Maryland Earned Income Tax Credit Assistance Program for
5 Low–Income Families.

6 (2) The purpose of the Program is to:

7 (i) identify residents who are eligible to claim the credit under §
8 10–704 of this article but have failed to claim the credit; and

9 (ii) provide residents identified under item (i) of this paragraph with
10 a streamlined mechanism to claim the credit under § 10–704 of this article.

11 (3) The Comptroller shall administer the Program.

12 (c) (1) This subsection applies to a taxable year beginning after December 31,
13 [2024] 2029.

14 (2) Subject to subsection (d) of this section, the Comptroller shall provide,
15 as part of the Program, a form to claim the credit under § 10–704 of this article to any
16 resident:

17 (i) for whom the Comptroller has received federal income tax return
18 information for a taxable year described in § 13–1104(c)(1) of this article;

19 (ii) whose wages were reported by the resident’s employer to the
20 Comptroller for that taxable year;

21 (iii) who the Comptroller determines, based on all available data,
22 may be eligible to claim the credit under § 10–704 of this article for that taxable year;

23 (iv) who failed to claim the credit under § 10–704 of this article for
24 that taxable year; and

25 (v) who is authorized to request a refund under § 13–1104(c)(1) of
26 this article.

27 (3) The Comptroller shall provide the form required under this subsection
28 no later than 45 days before the expiration of the statute of limitations for claiming a refund
29 under § 13–1104(c)(1) of this article.

30 (d) In processing the form to claim the credit under § 10–704 of this article, the
31 Comptroller shall calculate the credit as though the resident elected to use the standard
32 deduction under § 10–217 of this article to compute Maryland taxable income.

18 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:

19 (a) The Comptroller’s Office shall study and make recommendations regarding
20 outreach efforts to encourage eligible taxpayers to claim the State earned income tax credit.

21 (b) In conducting the study required under subsection (a) of this section, the
22 Comptroller’s Office shall:

23 (1) analyze the effectiveness of practices or forms of outreach in this State
24 and in other states to assist eligible taxpayers to claim the State earned income tax credit;

25 (2) analyze which Maryland demographic groups have responded well to
26 previous outreach efforts concerning the State earned income tax credit and which have
27 not responded well or remain underserved;

28 (3) analyze the degree to which and manner in which Maryland’s current
29 earned income tax credit outreach efforts are reliant on federal data sharing;

30 (4) research existing or possible methods to conduct outreach efforts in the
31 absence of federal data sharing;

1 (5) analyze how Maryland Tax Connect might simplify filing for the State
2 earned income tax credit and encourage more eligible taxpayers to claim the credit;

3 (6) research changes to the filing process that generally could simplify
4 filing or encourage more eligible taxpayers to claim the credit;

5 (7) research the potential for privacy risks if the Comptroller’s Office and
6 the Internal Revenue Service share data concerning the State earned income tax credit,
7 including whether the federal government might use the data shared by the Comptroller’s
8 Office for the purposes of immigration enforcement;

9 (8) identify and analyze strategies that are most effective in advising
10 eligible taxpayers who previously filed taxes but failed to claim the State earned income
11 tax credit of their ability to claim the credit in future filing years;

12 (9) analyze which strategies are most effective in identifying and reaching
13 low–income taxpayers who are not required to file an income tax return and encouraging
14 those taxpayers to file and claim the State earned income tax credit;

15 (10) analyze opportunities for partnership between the Comptroller’s Office
16 and State social service agencies for data sharing, outreach, and enhancing awareness of
17 the State earned income tax credit; and

18 (11) research whether employers who are required to notify their employees
19 about the State earned income tax credit need additional support or resources from the
20 Comptroller’s Office in carrying out that duty.

21 (c) On or before December 1, 2026, the Comptroller’s Office shall submit a report
22 of its findings and recommendations to the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257
23 of the State Government Article.

24 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:

28 tax credits and government assistance for which they may be eligible.

29 (b) On or before December 1, 2026, the Department of Service and Civic
30 Innovation shall submit a report on its findings and recommendations to the General
31 Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article.
