A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Information Technology – Establishment of the Office of Enterprise Data and
3 State Chief Data Officer and Collaboration With Agency Data Officers

4 FOR the purpose of establishing the position and responsibilities of the State Chief Data
5 Officer; establishing the Office of Enterprise Data in the Department of Information
6 Technology and the responsibilities of the Office; requiring certain units of State
7 government and authorizing certain units of State government to appoint an Agency
8 Data Officer and establishing the responsibilities of Agency Data Officers; and
9 generally relating to ensuring the efficient, secure, and ethical use of data in support
10 of State goals and initiatives.

12 Article – State Finance and Procurement
13 Section 3.5–319

10 (3) “DATA GOVERNANCE” MEANS THE AUTHORITY AND
11 DECISION–MAKING PARAMETERS ESTABLISHED FOR DATA MANAGEMENT,
12 LITERACY, AND USE THROUGHOUT ITS LIFE CYCLE TO ENSURE THAT THE STATE’S
13 DATA ASSETS ARE ACCURATE, CONSISTENT, SECURE, AND ACCESSIBLE WHILE
14 MANAGING PRIVACY AND CYBERSECURITY RISKS, INCLUDING CREATING POLICIES
15 AND PROCEDURES FOR DATA OWNERSHIP, ACCESS CONTROLS, SECURITY,
16 INTEGRITY, TRAINING, AND COMPLIANCE WITH INTERNAL AND EXTERNAL
17 REGULATIONS.

18 (4) “DATA LIFE CYCLE” MEANS THE FULL LIFE SPAN OF A DATA ASSET
19 FROM CREATION, COLLECTION, AND STORAGE THROUGH USE, SHARING,
20 ARCHIVING, AND DELETION, CONSISTENT WITH NIST–COMPLIANT STANDARDS
21 ESTABLISHED BY THE OFFICE.

22 (5) “DATA LITERACY” MEANS THE ABILITY TO UNDERSTAND,
23 INTERPRET, CRITICALLY EVALUATE, AND EFFECTIVELY COMMUNICATE DATA TO
24 INFORM DECISIONS AND DRIVE ACTION.

25 (6) “DATA MANAGEMENT” MEANS PRACTICES, PROCESSES,
26 POLICIES, AND TOOLS USED TO COLLECT, STORE, ORGANIZE, SECURE, MAINTAIN,
27 AND UTILIZE DATA EFFECTIVELY THROUGHOUT ITS ENTIRE LIFE CYCLE.

28 (7) “DATA QUALITY” MEANS HOW WELL DATA MEETS DEFINED
29 STANDARDS, INCLUDING ACCURACY, RELIABILITY, CONSISTENCY, TIMELINESS,
30 VALIDITY, AND UNIQUENESS.

31 (8) “DATA READINESS” MEANS DATA’S FITNESS FOR USE WITH
32 EMERGING TECHNOLOGIES SUCH AS ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE AND QUANTUM
33 COMPUTING.

7 (B) (1) THERE IS A STATE CHIEF DATA OFFICER IN THE DEPARTMENT.

8 (2) THE STATE CHIEF DATA OFFICER SHALL BE APPOINTED AND
9 SUPERVISED BY THE SECRETARY.

10 (3) THE RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE STATE CHIEF DATA OFFICER
11 INCLUDE:

12 (I) ESTABLISHING, COORDINATING, AND IMPLEMENTING THE
13 STATEWIDE DATA STRATEGY AND POLICIES FOR UNITS OF STATE GOVERNMENT;

14 (II) ENSURING RESPONSIBLE GOVERNANCE, USE, AND
15 MANAGEMENT OF STATE DATA;

16 (III) PROVIDING ADVICE, RECOMMENDATIONS, AND
17 CONSULTATIONS TO THE SECRETARY AND ANY UNIT OF STATE GOVERNMENT
18 CONCERNING DATA POLICIES, PROCEDURES, AND STANDARDS;

19 (IV) PUBLISHING AN ANNUAL STATE DATA STRATEGIC PLAN;

20 (V) DEVELOPING GUIDELINES FOR RESPONSIBLE AND
21 EQUITABLE USE OF DATA WITH CRITICAL AND EMERGING TECHNOLOGIES,
22 INCLUDING ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, AND REQUIREMENTS FOR TRANSPARENCY,
23 RISK MANAGEMENT, DATA MINIMIZATION, AND BIAS MITIGATION; AND

24 (VI) AT LEAST QUARTERLY, CONVENING AN ENTERPRISE DATA
25 COUNCIL CONSISTING OF THE AGENCY DATA OFFICERS APPOINTED UNDER
26 SUBSECTION (E) OR (F) OF THIS SECTION TO COORDINATE STATEWIDE DATA
27 GOVERNANCE STRUCTURES AND SHARE BEST PRACTICES.

28 (C) (1) THERE IS AN OFFICE OF ENTERPRISE DATA IN THE
29 DEPARTMENT.

30 (2) THE HEAD OF THE OFFICE IS THE STATE CHIEF DATA OFFICER.
