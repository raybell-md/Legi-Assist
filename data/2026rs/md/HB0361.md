17 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

18 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings

19 1–501.

20 The circuit courts are the highest common–law and equity courts of record exercising
21 original jurisdiction within the State. Each has full common–law and equity powers and
22 jurisdiction in all civil and criminal cases within its county, and all the additional powers
23 and jurisdiction conferred by the Constitution and by law, except where by law jurisdiction
24 has been limited or conferred exclusively upon another tribunal.
