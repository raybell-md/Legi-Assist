A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Family Law – Marriage License Applications – Appearance in Court

3 FOR the purpose of requiring both parties to be married, rather than one party, to appear
4 before the clerk of a circuit court to apply for a marriage license; and generally
5 relating to marriage license applications.

17 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

18 Article – Family Law

1 (1) appear before the clerk and give, under oath, the following information,
2 which shall be placed on an application form by the clerk:

3 (i) the full legal name of each party;

4 (ii) the place of residence of each party;

5 (iii) the date of birth of each party;

6 (iv) whether the parties are related by blood or marriage and, if so,
7 in which degree of relationship;

8 (v) the marital status of each party; and

9 (vi) whether either party was married previously, and the date and
10 place of each death or judicial determination that ended any former marriage;

11 (2) sign the application form; and

12 (3) provide the clerk with:

13 (i) the Social Security number of each party who has a Social
14 Security number; and

15 (ii) a copy of an official government–issued birth certificate or other
16 official government–issued document or record demonstrating the age of each party.

17 (d) (1) If the parties to be married are not residents of the county where the
18 marriage ceremony is to be performed, the clerk shall accept, instead of the application
19 specified in subsection (b) of this section, an affidavit from one of the parties to be married.

20 (2) The affidavit required under paragraph (1) of this subsection shall:

21 (i) contain the information required by subsection (b) of this section;
22 and

23 (ii) be sworn to under oath before a clerk or other comparable official
24 in the county, state, province, or country where the party resides.

25 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
26 October 1, 2026.
