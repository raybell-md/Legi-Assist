A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Reentry and Reintegration Support Program – Establishment

3 FOR the purpose of establishing a coordinated, interagency partnership among the
4 Department of Human Services, the Department of Public Safety and Correctional
5 Services, and the Maryland Department of Health to create a voluntary prerelease
6 benefits and identification application process for covered benefits for individuals
7 being released from State correctional facilities; and generally relating to reentry
8 and reintegration support for individuals being released from State correctional
9 facilities.

10 BY adding to
11 Article – Human Services
12 Section 5–324
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

18 5–324.

20 INDICATED.

1 (I) THE SUPPLEMENTAL NUTRITION ASSISTANCE PROGRAM;

2 (II) TEMPORARY CASH ASSISTANCE; AND

3 (III) THE MARYLAND MEDICAL ASSISTANCE PROGRAM.

4 (3) “DEPARTMENTS” MEANS THE DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN
5 SERVICES, THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC SAFETY AND CORRECTIONAL SERVICES,
6 AND THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH.

7 (4) “PROGRAM” MEANS THE MARYLAND REENTRY AND
8 REINTEGRATION SUPPORT PROGRAM.

9 (5) “RELEASING INSTITUTION” MEANS A STATE CORRECTIONAL
10 FACILITY OPERATED BY OR UNDER THE AUTHORITY OF THE MARYLAND
11 DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC SAFETY AND CORRECTIONAL SERVICES.

12 (6) “RETURNING CITIZEN” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL CONFINED IN A
13 STATE CORRECTIONAL FACILITY WHO IS EXPECTED TO BE RELEASED WITHIN 90
14 DAYS.

15 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENTS JOINTLY SHALL ESTABLISH AND ADMINISTER
16 THE MARYLAND REENTRY AND REINTEGRATION SUPPORT PROGRAM.

17 (2) THE PROGRAM SHALL PROVIDE COORDINATED PRERELEASE
18 COVERED BENEFITS APPLICATION ASSISTANCE TO RETURNING CITIZENS WHO
19 CHOOSE TO PARTICIPATE IN THE PROGRAM.

20 (C) (1) THE PROGRAM SHALL INCLUDE A VOLUNTARY AND
21 STANDARDIZED PRERELEASE APPLICATION PROCESS FOR COVERED BENEFITS.

22 (2) THE DEPARTMENTS JOINTLY SHALL DEVELOP A PROCESS TO
23 PROVIDE A RETURNING CITIZEN WITH THE OPPORTUNITY TO APPLY FOR COVERED
24 BENEFITS BEFORE THE RETURNING CITIZEN’S SCHEDULED RELEASE DATE.

25 (3) PROGRAM STAFF WITHIN THE RELEASING INSTITUTION SHALL
26 ASSIST A PARTICIPATING RETURNING CITIZEN IN COMPLETING AND SUBMITTING
27 ALL NECESSARY APPLICATIONS AND DOCUMENTATION FOR COVERED BENEFITS.

28 (D) (1) THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC SAFETY AND CORRECTIONAL
29 SERVICES SHALL ASSIST EACH PARTICIPATING RETURNING CITIZEN IN APPLYING
30 FOR COVERED BENEFITS AND OBTAINING REQUIRED VERIFICATIONS AT NO COST TO
31 THE INDIVIDUAL.

1 (2) REQUIRED VERIFICATIONS INCLUDE:

2 (I) A CERTIFIED COPY OF THE RETURNING CITIZEN’S BIRTH
3 CERTIFICATE;

4 (II) A COPY OF THE RETURNING CITIZEN’S SOCIAL SECURITY
5 CARD;

6 (III) PROOF OF THE RETURNING CITIZEN’S INCOME AND ASSETS;

7 (IV) PROOF OF THE RETURNING CITIZEN’S SHELTER COSTS; AND

8 (V) OTHER INFORMATION NECESSARY TO ESTABLISH
9 ELIGIBILITY FOR BENEFITS.

10 (3) ASSISTANCE UNDER THIS SUBSECTION SHALL BE PROVIDED IN A
11 TIMELY MANNER TO ENSURE, TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE, THAT THE RETURNING
12 CITIZEN POSSESSES THE REQUIRED VERIFICATIONS ON RELEASE FROM A STATE
13 CORRECTIONAL FACILITY.

14 (E) (1) ON OR BEFORE APRIL 1, 2027, THE DEPARTMENTS JOINTLY
15 SHALL DEVELOP AND ENTER INTO A MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING TO
16 IMPLEMENT THIS SECTION.

17 (2) THE MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING SHALL ESTABLISH A
18 SECURE DATA SHARING PROCESS TO:

19 (I) FACILITATE THE TIMELY PROCESSING OF APPLICATIONS
20 FOR COVERED BENEFITS;

21 (II) VERIFY A RETURNING CITIZEN’S IDENTITY AND
22 ELIGIBILITY; AND

23 (III) ENSURE THAT, TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE, COVERED
24 BENEFITS ARE ACTIVE AND ACCESSIBLE TO THE RETURNING CITIZEN IMMEDIATELY
25 ON RELEASE.

26 (3) DATA SHARED BETWEEN THE DEPARTMENTS UNDER THIS
27 SECTION SHALL BE USED SOLELY FOR THE PURPOSES OF ADMINISTERING THE
28 PROGRAM AND SHALL BE SUBJECT TO ALL APPLICABLE STATE AND FEDERAL LAWS.
