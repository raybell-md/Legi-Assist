A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Higher Education – Douglas J. J. Peters Veterans of the Afghanistan and Iraq
3 Conflicts Scholarship – Repeal of Termination Date

4 FOR the purpose of repealing the prohibition on awarding the Douglas J. J. Peters Veterans
5 of the Afghanistan and Iraq Conflicts Scholarship after a certain date; repealing the
6 limitation on renewing the Douglas J. J. Peters Veterans of the Afghanistan and Iraq
7 Conflicts Scholarship if the scholarship recipient received an initial scholarship
8 before a certain date; and generally relating to the Douglas J. J. Peters Veterans of
9 the Afghanistan and Iraq Conflicts Scholarship.

10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
11 Article – Education
12 Section 18–604
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

20 conflict if the individual was an active service member who served in:

21 (1) Afghanistan or contiguous air space, as defined in federal regulations,
22 on or after October 24, 2001; or

5 (c) An individual may apply to the Office for a scholarship under this section if
6 the individual:

7 (1) Is a resident of Maryland;

8 (2) (i) Is accepted for admission or enrolled in the regular
9 undergraduate program at an eligible institution; or

10 (ii) Is accepted for admission or enrolled in a 2–year terminal
11 certificate program in which the course work is acceptable for transfer credit for an
12 accredited baccalaureate program in an eligible institution; and

13 (3) (i) 1. Is a veteran who served in the Afghanistan or Iraq conflict;

14 2. Is an active service member who served in the Afghanistan
15 or Iraq conflict; or

16 3. Is a member of a reserve component who was activated as
17 a result of the Afghanistan or Iraq conflict described in subsection (a) of this section; or

18 (ii) Is a son, daughter, stepson, stepdaughter, or spouse of:

19 1. A veteran or an active service member who is serving or
20 has served in the Afghanistan or Iraq conflict; or

21 2. A member of the reserve component who was activated as
22 a result of the Afghanistan or Iraq conflict described in subsection (a) of this section.

23 (d) A scholarship awarded under this section may supplement, but is not
24 contingent on, any federal education benefits for which a recipient qualifies as a result of
25 an individual’s service or status as a dependent of an active service member or of a veteran.

26 (e) (1) Each scholarship may be used, at any eligible institution, to pay for
27 educational expenses as defined by the Office, including:

28 (i) Tuition and mandatory fees; and

29 (ii) Room and board.

30 (2) The annual amount of a scholarship may not exceed 50% of the
31 equivalent annual tuition, mandatory fees, and room and board of a resident
