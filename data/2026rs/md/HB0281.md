A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Family Law – Child Care Providers – Criminal Background Investigations

3 FOR the purpose of altering provisions relating to criminal background investigations for
4 certain individuals who care for or supervise children; requiring certain facilities to
5 apply to the Criminal Justice Information System Central Repository for a national
6 and State criminal history records check for each employee, employer, individual, or
7 volunteer; altering provisions relating to the process under which a certain State or
8 local law enforcement agency conducts a certain name–based check on certain
9 individuals; and generally relating to child care and criminal background
10 investigations.

5 5–555.

6 (a) The Department shall conduct the criminal history records checks required
7 under this Part V of this subtitle.

8 (b) (1) The facilities identified in § 5–551(a) of this subtitle shall apply to the
9 Central Repository for a national and State criminal history records check for each
10 employee, employer, individual, or volunteer.

11 (2) As part of an application for a criminal history records check, a facility
12 identified in § 5–551(a) of this subtitle shall submit to the Central Repository:

13 (i) a legible set of the employee’s, employer’s, individual’s, or
14 volunteer’s fingerprints taken in a format approved by the Director of the Central
15 Repository and the Director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation;

16 (ii) the fee authorized under § 10–221(b)(7) of the Criminal
17 Procedure Article for access to State criminal history records; and

18 (iii) the mandatory processing fee required by the Federal Bureau of
19 Investigation for a national criminal history records check.

20 (3) In accordance with §§ 10–201 through 10–229 of the Criminal
21 Procedure Article, the Central Repository shall forward the employee’s, employer’s,
22 individual’s, or volunteer’s [criminal history record information to]:

23 (i) STATE CRIMINAL HISTORY RECORD INFORMATION TO the
24 employee, employer, individual, or volunteer; and

25 (ii) NATIONAL CRIMINAL HISTORY RECORD INFORMATION TO
26 the authorized agencies required to license, register, approve, or certify the facility
27 identified in § 5–551(a) of this subtitle.

28 (c) The Department shall update an initial criminal history records check for an
29 employee, employer, or individual identified in § 5–551(b), (c), (d), or (e) of this subtitle and
30 issue an updated records check in accordance with this subsection.
