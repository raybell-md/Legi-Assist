1 (I) TITLE 11 OF THE COMMERCIAL LAW ARTICLE;

2 (II) TITLE 13 OF THE COMMERCIAL LAW ARTICLE; OR

3 (III) TITLE 11, SUBTITLE 3 OF THE CORPORATIONS AND
4 ASSOCIATIONS ARTICLE.

5 (B) THERE IS AN OFFICE OF THE ATTORNEY GENERAL ENFORCEMENT
6 RECOVERY FUND IN THE OFFICE.

7 (C) THE OFFICE SHALL ADMINISTER THE FUND.

8 (D) (1) THE FUND IS A SPECIAL, NONLAPSING FUND THAT IS NOT
9 SUBJECT TO § 7–302 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.

10 (2) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL HOLD THE FUND SEPARATELY,
11 AND THE COMPTROLLER SHALL ACCOUNT FOR THE FUND.

12 (E) THE FUND CONSISTS OF:

13 (1) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (H) OF THIS SECTION, MONEY
14 DEPOSITED FROM QUALIFIED RECOVERIES; AND

15 (2) ANY OTHER MONEY FROM ANY OTHER SOURCE ACCEPTED FOR
16 THE BENEFIT OF THE FUND.

17 (F) THE FUND MAY BE USED ONLY TO SUPPORT THE CONSUMER
18 PROTECTION, ANTITRUST, AND SECURITIES ENFORCEMENT ACTIVITIES OF THE
19 OFFICE, INCLUDING:

20 (1) INVESTIGATION AND PROSECUTION OF VIOLATIONS;

21 (2) EXPERT WITNESS FEES AND CONSULTING SERVICES;

22 (3) TECHNOLOGY AND DATA ANALYTICS TOOLS;

23 (4) TRAINING AND PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT FOR
24 ENFORCEMENT STAFF;

25 (5) PUBLIC EDUCATION AND OUTREACH REGARDING CONSUMER
26 RIGHTS; AND

27 (6) ANY OTHER DIRECT ENFORCEMENT–RELATED EXPENSES.

1 (G) (1) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL INVEST THE MONEY OF THE FUND
2 IN THE SAME MANNER AS OTHER STATE MONEY MAY BE INVESTED.

3 (2) INTEREST EARNINGS OF THE FUND SHALL BE CREDITED TO THE
4 GENERAL FUND OF THE STATE.

5 (H) (1) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY DEPOSIT INTO THE FUND UP TO
6 25% OF EACH QUALIFIED RECOVERY NOT TO EXCEED $7,500,000 PER FISCAL YEAR
7 IN AGGREGATE DEPOSITS FROM ALL QUALIFIED RECOVERIES.

8 (2) DEPOSITS UNDER THIS SUBSECTION MAY BE MADE ONLY FROM
9 QUALIFIED RECOVERIES THAT:

10 (I) ARE OBTAINED THROUGH JUDGMENTS, SETTLEMENTS, OR
11 CONSENT DECREES;

12 (II) EXCEED $100,000 IN TOTAL RECOVERY AMOUNTS; AND

13 (III) ARE NOT OTHERWISE SPECIFICALLY DESIGNATED BY
14 COURT ORDER, SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT, OR STATE LAW FOR ALTERNATIVE
15 PURPOSES, INCLUDING RESTITUTION FOR VICTIMS.

16 (3) ANY AMOUNT DEPOSITED INTO THE FUND UNDER THIS SECTION:

17 (I) IS SUPPLEMENTAL TO AND IS NOT INTENDED TO TAKE THE
18 PLACE OF FUNDING THAT WOULD OTHERWISE BE APPROPRIATED TO THE OFFICE IN
19 THE STATE BUDGET; AND

20 (II) MAY NOT REDUCE AMOUNTS AVAILABLE FOR RESTITUTION
21 TO VICTIMS OR OTHER REMEDIAL PURPOSES.

22 (4) MONEY IN THE FUND MAY NOT BE USED FOR RESTITUTION OR
23 OTHER PAYMENTS TO VICTIMS OR THIRD PARTIES.

24 (5) THE EXISTENCE OF THE FUND MAY NOT BE USED AS A BASIS TO
25 REDUCE APPROPRIATIONS TO THE OFFICE BELOW THE LEVEL THAT WOULD
26 OTHERWISE BE PROVIDED.

27 (I) (1) AT THE END OF EACH FISCAL YEAR, IF THE BALANCE OF THE
28 FUND EXCEEDS $7,500,000, THE COMPTROLLER SHALL TRANSFER THE EXCESS
29 BALANCE TO THE GENERAL FUND OF THE STATE.
