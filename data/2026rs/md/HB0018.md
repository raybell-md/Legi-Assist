A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Transportation Network Companies – Transportation Network Operators –
3 Minimum Payments for Passenger Trips

4 FOR the purpose of requiring a transportation network company to pay operators, after a
5 certain date and for any passenger trip originating in the State, a certain minimum
6 amount per mile and per minute, an additional amount per mile for an operator
7 using a wheelchair accessible vehicle, a certain minimum amount for any passenger
8 trip, and at least a certain percentage of any cancellation fee charged by a
9 transportation network company; adjusting the minimum amount paid per mile and
10 per minute by inflation each year; and generally relating to compensation paid to
11 transportation network operators by transportation network companies.

1 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.

2 (l) “Transportation network company” means a company that operates in the
3 State using a digital network to connect passengers to transportation network operators or
4 transportation network partners for transportation network services.

5 (m) “Transportation network operator”, “transportation network partner”, or
6 “transportation network driver” means an individual who:

7 (1) has been issued a transportation network operator’s license, or is
8 otherwise authorized, by the Commission to provide transportation network services;

9 (2) receives, through a transportation network company’s digital network
10 application, a connection to a potential passenger to transport the passenger between
11 points chosen by the passenger in exchange for the payment of a fee to the transportation
12 network company; and

13 (3) uses a motor vehicle that is owned, leased, or otherwise authorized for
14 use by the individual and is approved for use in providing transportation network services
15 by the Commission.

16 (n) (1) “Transportation network services” means the activities of an operator
17 during:

18 (i) transportation network coverage period one, during which the
19 operator is logged onto and ready to accept a prearranged ride request made through a
20 transportation network company’s digital network application;

21 (ii) transportation network coverage period two, during which the
22 operator accepts a ride request from a passenger that is prearranged through a
23 transportation network company’s digital network application, and is traveling to a
24 predetermined location to pick up the passenger; and

25 (iii) transportation network coverage period three, during which the
26 operator transports the passenger and continuing until the passenger departs the motor
27 vehicle.

28 (2) “Transportation network services” does not include:

29 (i) providing taxicab services, sedan services, or limousine services;

30 (ii) any shared expense carpool arrangement or service or other type
31 of arrangement or service in which a driver receives a fee that does not exceed the driver’s
32 costs associated with providing a ride; or
