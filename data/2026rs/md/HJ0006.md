HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION

2 A House Joint Resolution concerning

3 Judicial Compensation Commission – Recommendations

4 FOR the purpose of establishing the compensation of the members of the Judiciary in this
5 State in accordance with Section 1–708 of the Courts and Judicial Proceedings
6 Article of the Annotated Code of Maryland.

7 WHEREAS, Section 1–708(b)(2) of the Courts and Judicial Proceedings Article of the
8 Annotated Code of Maryland establishes a seven–member Judicial Compensation
9 Commission appointed by the Governor with two members appointed on nomination of the
10 President of the Senate, two members appointed on nomination of the Speaker of the House
11 of Delegates, one member appointed on nomination of the Maryland State Bar Association,
12 and two members appointed at large. The Judicial Compensation Commission is
13 constituted as follows: appointments made on the nomination of the President of the
14 Senate: Meghan Casey and Carlos Williams; appointments made on the nomination of the
15 Speaker of the House of Delegates: Victoria Fretwell and John Wasilisin; appointment
16 made on the nomination of the Maryland State Bar Association: Raphael Santini; and
17 appointments at large: Monica Mitchell and Chanel Mason. The Commission members
18 elected Meghan Casey to serve as the chair of the Commission. The Commission is charged
19 with reviewing the salaries and pensions of the judges of the Judiciary of Maryland and
20 making written recommendations to the Governor and the General Assembly on or after
21 September 1, 2011, September 1, 2013, and every 4 years thereafter; and

22 WHEREAS, Section 1–708(d) of the Courts and Judicial Proceedings Article of the
23 Annotated Code of Maryland provides as follows: the General Assembly may amend this
24 Joint Resolution to decrease any of the Commission’s salary recommendations, but no
25 reduction may diminish the salary of a judge during the judge’s continuance in office. The
26 General Assembly may not amend this Joint Resolution to increase these recommended
27 salaries. Should the General Assembly not adopt or amend this Joint Resolution within 50
28 days after its introduction, the salaries recommended herein shall apply during fiscal years
29 2027 through 2030. Should the General Assembly reject any or all of the salaries herein
30 recommended, the salaries of the judges so affected shall remain unchanged during fiscal
31 years 2027 through 2030 unless modified under other provisions of the law; and
