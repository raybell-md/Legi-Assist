A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Adult Prison School Board Model Development Committee

3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Adult Prison School Board Model Development
4 Committee; and generally relating to the Adult Prison School Board Model
5 Development Committee.

6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That:

8 (a) There is an Adult Prison School Board Model Development Committee.

9 (b) The Committee consists of the following members:

10 (1) one member of the Senate of Maryland, appointed by the President of
11 the Senate;

12 (2) one member of the House of Delegates, appointed by the Speaker of the
13 House;

14 (3) the Secretary of Public Safety and Correctional Services, or the
15 Secretary’s designee;

16 (4) the State Superintendent of Schools, or the State Superintendent’s
17 designee;

18 (5) the Secretary of Higher Education, or the Secretary’s designee;

1 (6) the Superintendent for the Juvenile Services Education Program, or the
2 Superintendent’s designee;

3 (7) the Secretary of Labor, or the Secretary’s designee; and

4 (8) the following members appointed by the Governor:

5 (i) a representative of Maryland Correctional Enterprises;

6 (ii) a national expert on correctional education issues; and

7 (iii) a representative of a nonprofit organization focused on
8 correctional reform.

9 (c) The Governor shall appoint the chair of the Committee.

10 (d) The State Department of Education shall provide staff for the Committee.

11 (e) A member of the Committee:

12 (1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Committee; but

13 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
14 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.

15 (f) The Committee shall:

16 (1) develop a comprehensive plan for a school board system to oversee
17 correctional education for individuals subject to the criminal and juvenile justice systems;
18 and

19 (2) conduct a fiscal mapping project for correctional education that
20 examines the needs and options necessary to sustain a school board system, including:

21 (i) examining internal and external funding sources;

22 (ii) determining funding required for teacher salaries, professional
23 development, educational services and supplies, and any other costs associated with
24 developing and implementing the school board system; and

25 (iii) developing an independent relationship with the Department of
26 Budget and Management to establish and manage capital and operating budgets.

27 (g) The school board system plan developed under subsection (f) of this section
28 shall include:
