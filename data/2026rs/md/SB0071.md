A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Education – Interjurisdictional Safe Pathways Commission – Establishment

3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Interjurisdictional Safe Pathways Commission to
4 identify, coordinate, and secure funding for safe pathways for students traveling to
5 and from school; and generally relating to the Interjurisdictional Safe Pathways
6 Commission.

7 BY adding to
8 Article – Education
9 Section 7–811
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

14 Article – Education

15 7–811.

16 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
17 INDICATED.

18 (2) “COMMISSION” MEANS THE INTERJURISDICTIONAL SAFE
19 PATHWAYS COMMISSION.

20 (3) “IEP” MEANS AN INDIVIDUALIZED EDUCATION PROGRAM AS
21 DEFINED UNDER THE FEDERAL INDIVIDUALS WITH DISABILITIES EDUCATION ACT.

1 (4) (I) “SAFE PATHWAYS” MEANS PHYSICAL INFRASTRUCTURE TO
2 ENSURE SAFE ROUTES FOR STUDENTS TRAVELING TO AND FROM SCHOOL,
3 INCLUDING SIDEWALKS, CROSSWALKS, TRAFFIC CALMING MEASURES, BIKE LANES,
4 PEDESTRIAN BRIDGES, AND SCHOOL ZONE SIGNAGE.

5 (II) “SAFE PATHWAYS” INCLUDES EDUCATIONAL PROGRAMS
6 AND POLICY INITIATIVES THAT PROMOTE SAFE ROUTES FOR STUDENTS TRAVELING
7 TO AND FROM SCHOOL.

8 (B) THERE IS AN INTERJURISDICTIONAL SAFE PATHWAYS COMMISSION.

9 (C) (1) THE COMMISSION CONSISTS OF THE FOLLOWING MEMBERS:

10 (I) THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT OF SCHOOLS, OR THE STATE
11 SUPERINTENDENT’S DESIGNEE;

12 (II) THE SECRETARY OF TRANSPORTATION, OR THE
13 SECRETARY’S DESIGNEE;

14 (III) THE SECRETARY OF HEALTH, OR THE SECRETARY’S
15 DESIGNEE;

16 (IV) THE SECRETARY OF DISABILITIES, OR THE SECRETARY’S
17 DESIGNEE; AND

18 (V) THE FOLLOWING MEMBERS, APPOINTED BY THE STATE
19 SUPERINTENDENT:

20 1. FOUR REPRESENTATIVES FROM LOCAL SCHOOL
21 SYSTEMS;

22 2. THREE REPRESENTATIVES FROM MUNICIPAL LOCAL
23 MANAGEMENT BOARDS;

24 3. ONE STUDENT WHO ATTENDS A PUBLIC SCHOOL IN
25 THE STATE;

26 4. ONE STUDENT WITH AN IEP WHO ATTENDS A PUBLIC

3 7. TWO MEMBERS WHO REPRESENT PRIVATE OR

6 (2) THE GOVERNOR SHALL DESIGNATE A CHAIR OF THE COMMISSION
7 FROM AMONG ITS MEMBERS.

8 (3) EACH MEMBER SERVES FOR A TERM OF 3 YEARS.

9 (4) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE STAFF AND OTHER
10 NECESSARY SUPPORT TO THE COMMISSION.

11 (5) A MEMBER OF THE COMMISSION MAY NOT RECEIVE
12 COMPENSATION FOR SERVING ON THE COMMISSION, BUT IS ENTITLED TO
13 REIMBURSEMENT FOR EXPENSES UNDER THE STANDARD STATE TRAVEL
14 REGULATIONS, AS PROVIDED IN THE STATE BUDGET.

15 (6) THE COMMISSION SHALL MEET AT LEAST FOUR TIMES A YEAR, OR
16 AS NECESSARY TO FULFILL ITS DUTIES.

17 (D) (1) THE PURPOSE OF THE COMMISSION IS TO IDENTIFY,
18 COORDINATE, AND SECURE FUNDING FOR SAFE PATHWAYS FOR STUDENTS
19 TRAVELING TO AND FROM SCHOOL.

20 (2) THE COMMISSION SHALL COLLABORATE WITH LOCAL
21 GOVERNMENTS, STATE AGENCIES, AND RELEVANT STAKEHOLDERS TO DEVELOP
22 INFRASTRUCTURE, PROGRAMS, AND GRANT STRATEGIES.

23 (E) THE COMMISSION SHALL:

24 (1) CONDUCT AN INVENTORY OF CURRENT SAFE PATHWAYS FOR
25 STUDENT TRAVEL;

26 (2) IDENTIFY GAPS IN SAFE PATHWAYS INFRASTRUCTURE AND
27 PROGRAMS;

28 (3) EVALUATE FUNDING NEEDS AND OPPORTUNITIES, INCLUDING
29 FEDERAL GRANTS, STATE TRANSPORTATION FUNDS, PRIVATE GRANTS, AND
30 PHILANTHROPIC CONTRIBUTIONS;
