Howard County - Alcoholic Beverages - Class D Licenses - Delivery 
 
 
This bill authorizes the holder of a Class D license or an employee of the license holder to 
deliver alcoholic beverages within Howard County in the same manner as the holder of a 
Class A license. The bill takes effect July 1, 2025.  
   
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None.  
  
Local Effect:  Howard County finances and operations are not materially affected.      
  
Small Business Effect:  Meaningful. Small businesses with a Class D license that are able 
to deliver alcoholic beverages under the bill are likely to experience increased sales and 
revenues.      
  
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  Class D alcoholic beverages licenses generally authorize the sale of 
alcoholic beverages for consumption on- and off-premises consumption only.  
 
Chapter 662 of 2021 authorized the holder of a Class A license or an employee of the 
license holder to deliver alcoholic beverages within Howard County. An employee making 
a delivery must be at least 18 years old. The deliverer and recipient must both sign a form 
indicating that (1) the recipient claimed to be 21 years old and presented documentation to 
that effect; (2) the recipient knows it is a criminal offense for alcoholic beverages to be
