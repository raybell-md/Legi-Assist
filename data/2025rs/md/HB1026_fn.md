Frederick County - Alcoholic Beverages - Tasting Permits 
 
 
This bill converts Frederick Countyâ€™s beer and wine consumption permit and its beer, wine, 
and liquor consumption permit into a beer and wine tasting permit, and a beer, wine, and 
liquor tasting permit, respectively. The bill removes specified limitations on the size and 
quantity of sample servings of beer, wine, and liquor and instead requires the 
Frederick County Board of License Commissioners to regulate the quantity of beer, wine, 
and liquor that can be served by a permit holder to an individual and the number of bottles 
or other containers from which the quantity is served. The bill takes effect July 1, 2025. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. 
  
Local Effect:  The bill does not materially affect Frederick County finances or operations. 
 
Small Business Effect:  Minimal. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  The board may issue a beer and wine consumption permit or a beer, wine, 
and liquor consumption permit to a holder of an appropriate Class A license. 
A consumption permit authorizes a license holder to serve specified alcoholic beverages 
for on-premises consumption. An establishment with a consumption permit may allow an 
individual to consume a single serving of 12 total ounces of beer or 6 total ounces of wine, 
and for liquor permit holders, 1.5 total ounces of liquor. Alternatively, an establishment 
can allow an individual to consume three individual sample servings of beer and wine (and 
liquor, when applicable) in any combination, each consisting of not more than 2 ounces of
