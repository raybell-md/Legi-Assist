Business Occupations and Professions - Professional Engineers - Examination 
 
 
This bill requires an applicant for licensure as a professional engineer under the 
work-experience-only option to take and pass the fundamentals of engineering examination 
prior to taking the principles and practice of engineering examination.

Analysis 
 
Current Law:  The State Board for Professional Engineers in the Maryland Department 
of Labor licenses and regulates professional engineers. In addition to other requirements, 
an individual may qualify for licensure as a professional engineer by meeting one of 
three combinations of educational and experience requirements. Each of the three options 
requires a specified amount of work experience in engineering that is satisfactory to the 
board, depending on the level of educational attainment. Generally, the work experience 
requirement is either 4, 8, or 12 years, with progressively more work experience required 
as educational attainment decreases.
