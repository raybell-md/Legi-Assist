Public Health - Health Equity Dashboard 
 
 
This bill requires the Maryland Department of Health (MDH), in collaboration with the 
Maryland Commission on Health Equity (MCHE), to develop a clear and 
easy-to-understand graphic data dashboard that includes age-adjusted health disparity data 
disaggregated by race, ethnicity, and gender. The data dashboard must include information 
on health insurance access, cardiovascular disease, chronic disease, mental health and 
substance use, cancer, HIV/AIDS, sexually transmitted infection, body mass index, and 
any other health indicators determined relevant by MDH. MDH must update the data 
dashboard on a quarterly basis, as determined by data availability, and post the most recent 
updated data dashboard prominently on its website. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  MDH, in collaboration with MCHE, can develop and update a health equity 
data dashboard using existing budgeted resources. Revenues are not affected. 
  
Local Effect:  None. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None.

Among other things, MCHE is responsible for determining the impact of the following 
factors on the health of Maryland residents:  access to safe and affordable housing; 
educational attainment; opportunities for employment; economic stability; environmental 
factors; public safety, as specified; and food insecurity. MCHE must develop and monitor 
a statewide health equity plan as required by the Center for Medicare and Medicaid 
Innovation under any agreement entered into between the State and the Centers for 
Medicare and Medicaid Services. MCHE must, in coordination with the State-designated 
health information exchange, establish an advisory committee to make recommendations 
on data collection, needs, quality, reporting, evaluation, and visualization for the 
commission to carry out its work.
