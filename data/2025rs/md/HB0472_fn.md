Baltimore City - Alcoholic Beverages - 45th Alcoholic Beverages District - 
Licenses 
 
 
This bill authorizes the Baltimore City Board of License Commissioners to issue a Class C 
beer, wine, and liquor license in the 400 block of East Oliver Street in the 45th alcoholic 
beverages district, as specified. The bill also establishes a geographic boundary in the 
45th alcoholic beverages district within which a Class A beer, wine, and liquor license 
holder or a Class B-D-7 beer, wine, and liquor license holder may only sell alcoholic 
beverages during specified hours. The bill takes effect July 1, 2025. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. 
 
Local Effect:  The bill does not materially affect Baltimore City finances or operations. 
 
Small Business Effect:  Minimal. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Bill Summary:  This bill authorizes the board to issue a Class C beer, wine, and liquor 
license in the 400 block of East Oliver Street in the 45th alcoholic beverages district only 
if (1) the applicant does not convert the license to a different license class; (2) alcoholic 
beverages are served outdoors only to patrons seated at tables or standing in a courtyard 
area; (3) the applicant does not allow the off-premises sale of alcoholic beverages; 
(4) alcoholic beverages sales do not begin earlier than 10:00 a.m.; and (5) the applicant 
executes a memorandum of understanding with the Greenmount West Community 
Association.

Additionally, the bill authorizes the holders of a Class A beer, wine, and liquor license or 
a Class B-D-7 beer, wine, and liquor license to sell beer, wine, and liquor from 9:00 a.m. 
to 10:00 p.m. in a specified area. For a license holder within the bounded area, the hours 
of sale (1) may not begin before 9:00 a.m. or end after 10:00 p.m. and (2) may not be 
extended if they begin later than 9:00 a.m. or end before 10:00 p.m. 
 
Current Law:  Generally, the board is prohibited from issuing new alcoholic beverage 
licenses in the 40th, 41st, 43rd, 44th, and 45th alcoholic beverages districts, except under 
specific circumstances. 
 
The board may issue a one-day license or a Class B beer, wine, and liquor license to 
restaurants in these districts if they meet a minimum capital investment of $200,000 
(excluding land and buildings) and have a seating capacity of at least 75 individuals. 
 
Additionally, Class C beer, wine, and liquor licenses may be issued in certain locations 
within the 40th, 44th, and 45th districts. Several Class B-D-7 licenses may also be issued 
in designated areas, provided applicants meet food sales requirements, execute 
memorandums of understanding with community associations, and comply with other 
licensing conditions. 
 
A Class A beer, wine, and liquor license holder may sell alcohol Monday through Saturday 
from 9:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. in the Park Heights Master Plan area and from 6 a.m. to 
midnight elsewhere in the city. On select Sundays between Thanksgiving and  
New Yearâ€™s Day, sales are permitted from 1:00 p.m. to 9:00 p.m., provided the licensee 
pays a supplementary fee of $120 per Sunday by September 30. Additionally, the license 
holder may sell on two other Sundays during the year by paying the same fee at least 
two weeks in advance.
